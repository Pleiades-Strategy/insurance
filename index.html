The Landscape

Why insurance legislation matters for climate

The insurance market is a leading indicator of where climate risk is landing hardest — and who bears the cost. State legislatures are the primary arena where that is being fought out.

🏠

The Affordability Crisis

Insurers are withdrawing from California, Florida, Louisiana, and other high-risk states. Homeowners face skyrocketing premiums or no coverage at all — threatening property values and mortgage markets.

⚖️

The Regulatory Response

States are debating rate regulation, climate risk disclosure requirements, and new consumer protections. The outcomes will determine whether the cost of climate risk is borne fairly — or shifted onto those least able to pay.

See rate regulation bills →
🌊

The Bigger Picture

The insurance crisis is a preview of a broader climate-driven financial crisis. Pleiades tracks how policymakers are responding — and whether those responses protect people or perpetuate risk.

Read our analysis →

Fortification & Resilience

A growing category of bills funds home hardening programs and building code upgrades designed to reduce vulnerability — and insurance costs — in disaster-prone areas.

Fossil Fuel Accountability

Some states are pursuing legislation to recover climate-related costs from fossil fuel companies. These bills face steep political headwinds but represent a significant emerging trend.

Climate Risk Disclosure

Zero of ten climate risk disclosure bills tracked this session have been enacted. This category reflects the gap between policy ambition and political feasibility at the state level.

2025 Session Findings

What the data shows

Key takeaways from our analysis of the 2025 legislative session on insurance and climate risk.

66

Consumer protection bills introduced

The largest single category in our tracker — but with only a 12% enactment rate, it reflects the gap between what consumers need and what legislators are willing to deliver. Most are dying in committee.

0/10

Systemic climate risk bills stalled

Bills addressing systemic climate-related financial risks — including insurer solvency, climate risk disclosure, and fossil fuel accountability — have an enactment rate near zero. The financial system's exposure to climate risk is growing faster than the policy response.

40

States actively legislating

This is no longer a coastal issue. Bills are active in 40 states — including Oklahoma, Alabama, Iowa, and Utah — reflecting the national spread of climate-related insurance stress into inland and traditionally lower-risk markets.

21%

Overall enactment rate — but unevenly distributed

One in five tracked bills became law, but the wins are concentrated in insurer-friendly categories like litigation reform and non-admitted market expansion. Consumer and climate accountability bills lag far behind.

About This Project

Built by Pleiades Strategy

Pleiades Strategy tracks state-level anti-ESG policy and climate finance legislation. Since 2022, we have maintained the most comprehensive database of state anti-ESG efforts, tracking their progress, their sponsors, and their real-world impacts on pensionholders, taxpayers, and investors.

The Insurance & Climate Risk Legislative Tracker is part of a broader effort to make our research accessible to advocates, journalists, policymakers, and the public. The bill tracking data is curated by our research team and synced automatically from our Airtable database.

This report was written by Connor Gibson, Frances Sawyer, and Jeremy Siegel for Pleiades Strategy.
